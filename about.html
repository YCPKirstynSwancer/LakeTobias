About Our Family Activities In Central Pennsylvania

From our establishment in 1965, Lake Tobias Wildlife Park has been focused on providing fun family activities and animal-themed attractions for local residents and central Pennsylvania visitors to enjoy.

Our Founder

Lake Tobias Wildlife Park was founded by J.R. Tobias as “just a hobby to fall back on in my retirement” and grew into a beloved local attraction over the decades. Originally, J.R.’s interest in wildlife and exotic creatures led him to consider agriculture and animals as a career, but he opted to join the Marines instead of going to college, enlisting in 1943. After leaving the Marines, J.R. Tobias went on to own multiple successful businesses, including a dairy, a cement block manufacturing company, a John Deere farm machinery business, and an excavating and grading company. The equipment and skills from these businesses came in handy when it was time to build the park. He constructed all of the stream-fed lakes that visitors enjoy today during visits to Lake Tobias Wildlife Park.
Before J.R. Tobias’ death, he transferred ownership and operation of the park to his children, and his six remaining children and a grandchild run the park today. This self-sustaining family attraction welcomes over 180,000 visitors a year. The park gets no state or federal funding, so revenue from the park covers all operating expenses.

Giraffes


Come meet our newest additions to the park – our three Reticulated Giraffes, Spike, Bakari, and Tucker. Take the trail through the newest section of the park to arrive at their new exhibit located adjacent to the safari tour. You’ll find them roaming in their pasture where they have over three acres of land to explore and play, or interact with park visitors. Giraffes are the tallest land animal on earth and getting to see these gravity-defying creatures up close is a unique experience that is not to be forgotten.

Close Encounter

Spike, Bakari, and Tucker also have their own elevated feeding platform. As a visitor you will be able to purchase special food for the giraffes and get up close and personal as they take the food right out of your hands.
Please note that the giraffes’ diet is very strict, even more so than other animals at the park. A limited supply of food will be sold during each feeding time so that our giraffes maintain a healthy weight. Food selection may vary by day and there may be periods of time when they are not taking snacks from visitors.

Times

The giraffe exhibit is open from 10:00 AM until closing. You can view the giraffes at any point during these hours However, feeding is only permitted during the feeding times listed below.
Giraffes tend to return to their indoor exhibit 30-60 minutes before park closing.

Giraffe Feeding Times

Visitors may feed the giraffes at 10:30, 1:00, and 4:00. Only food sold at the Giraffe Food Booth is permitted to feed the giraffes.
You can purchase various vegetables or Acacia branches for the giraffes to enjoy.

Safari Tour


About Our Safari Tour

If you’re looking for a fun and educational experience for the whole family, visit Lake Tobias Wildlife Park for our exciting Safari Tour. You will encounter exotic animals from all over the world while learning about their natural habitats. Our wildlife park has been bringing people face-to-face with animals from around the world since 1965.
Enjoy a ride in an open-air cruiser across 150 beautiful acres of rolling hills, pasture, and woodlands, where you’ll see rare and exotic animals from around the world. Our experienced tour guides will share their knowledge about the various species you encounter, as well as their habitats.

In 2015 we opened a new Safari Station to improve our guests’ safari experience. The new station features a larger loading area to accommodate more guests, as well as a snack shop and restrooms.
On our busiest days, we run ten tour buses on a continuous basis, allowing us to send out a new tour every 10-15 minutes.

Times

Safari tours begin when the park opens.
Tours last approximately 45 minutes.
Last tour goes out one hour before closing.
Tours are offered on a first-come, first-serve basis and depart multiple times per day.

What You'll See

When you and your family board our safari tour, you’ll be transported into another world. You’ll see herds of mammals and flightless birds of all shapes and sizes. With approximately 500 animals roaming freely, you’ll see something different every time you take the tour. Your family will be thrilled by how close the animals come to the tour bus—some will even allow you to touch them!

Petting Zoo

All parents want to create meaningful memories with their kids. For an experience your children will remember for years to come, visit the Petting Zoo at Lake Tobias Wildlife Park.
Children and adults alike will enjoy our collection of friendly creatures from all over the world. We have animals of all shapes and sizes, from the smallest African pygmy goats to long-necked alpacas. Visit everything from domestic farm animals and exotics like camels and alpacas. We take special care of our creatures, so they are happy and well-socialized. Our animals love attention, but keep in mind that they are not trained, so they may jump on you or nibble on your clothes. Also, note that some animals live in the petting zoo area but are not available for petting.

Pet and Feed

Like most attractions at Lake Tobias, visitors are welcome to feed the animals in the Petting Zoo.
Pellets and carrots can be purchased from employees at the petting zoo booth or from coin-operated dispensers located around the petting zoo and the rest of the park. The USDA carefully regulates these animals’ diets, so please keep in mind that only food sold at the park may be given to any of our zoo creatures. This is to protect them from accidentally being exposed to any outside contaminants.

Lions, Tigers and Bears

Many Lake Tobias visitors absolutely shudder with the anticipation of meeting the most fearsome species we display, including the magnificent African lion, one of the most distinctly recognizable predators on the planet. Not far behind is the Bengal tiger – with its unmistakable stripes and immense size, this creature has earned a formidable reputation, and Lake Tobias Wildlife Park offers a chance to see both of these big cats up close and personal.
The black bear is another favorite with its fair share of admirers. Spread far and wide across North America, these omnivorous creatures may not be as large as other species, but they can nonetheless reach an impressive stature. Often weighing over 500 lb. and sometimes measuring up to 6’ tall, they’re certainly a sight to behold.

Birds Everywhere

View some of the world’s most distinctive feathered fauna at Lake Tobias Wildlife Park. Drink in the sight of our ostriches, a bird that is both flightless and astonishingly large. Among our more elegant tenants is the East African crowned crane, which features an immediately identifiable golden crest that radiates royalty. While navigating the park, visitors may even catch this bird performing an energetic dance that is part of the species’ mating ritual. Equally elegant and famed for its stunning plumage, the peacock has long remained a staple of zoos across the globe, enchanting onlookers with its spread of iridescent feathers. The peacock, along with various chickens and guinea roam the park freely interacting with visitors.

Smaller Mammals

Lake Tobias is also home to many smaller mammals which have just as much personality as their larger relatives. Children of all ages enjoy the curious prairie dogs which enjoy popping up from their underground homes that they build in their specialized exhibit. You can also check out the largest rodent in the world, the Capybara, who peacefully swim in the lake and cautiously check out park visitors. A new addition, our Gibbons, have quickly become a park favorite due to their distinct singing and expressive faces.

Reptiles & Exotics Building

Touch an alligator, slide your hand across a python – the Reptiles & Exotics Facility will amaze you as a real crowd-pleaser exhibit at the park. Large, custom-designed habitats give you a glimpse into the dwellings of these rare and exotic creatures while educational presentations enlighten you. Our well-trained staff is eager to provide you with a memory-making experience.

Reptiles not your thing? The Reptiles & Exotics Building is also home to many amazing types of exotic mammals and tropical birds.

Sit or Stand

Stroll through the building at any time and you will see poison dart frogs, pythons, tamarins, marmoset, tropical birds, alligators, lizards, large tortoises, a two-toed tree sloth, and more.
Tired after a long day at the park? You can also sit to catch periodic 20 minute reptile shows where you will learn about fascinating animals and the zoo conservation efforts that are trying to save them. Some of the animals may even be brought out of their facilities so you can get a better look.

Baby Animal Program


At Lake Tobias Wildlife Park, we have a Baby Animal Program to help the babies in our care. While you are here, you will be able to meet the youngsters featured in the park exhibits. For more than half a century, we have been nestled in Halifax, PA with baby animals and their families.

Baby animals are one of the most popular reasons that folks come to the park each year, with the selection changing every season. The Baby Animal Crew helps care for littles who may have been rejected by the mother requiring our staff to step in and take over. Sometimes, the separation is temporary while the baby is being treated for an illness or needs help with early-detected nursing difficulties. And, other times it is to get the baby ready to be placed in another zoo. An animal that is hand-raised is much less stressed when transporting, when needing veterinary care and when transitioning to a new home.
We allow the baby animals time to socialize with guests while also tending to their food, relaxation and family socialization needs. Most of the little creatures may be touched and Baby Animal Crew members are on hand to answer your questions. You may also find a feeding schedule posted at the exhibit for you to get a glimpse of some of the youngsters while they eat. All baby animals are on a strict, regulated diet to ensure their health, so please do not feed the babies.

Other Baby Animal Sightings

In addition to the baby animals being cared for by the Baby Animal Crew, you can see young animals in exhibits with their parents in many cases. As you go through the park, make sure you find out which of our animals have youngsters for you to enjoy.
We have animals from six different continents, which means that you and your family will have the opportunity to see exotic creatures from afar as well as offerings from closer to home, such as the North American bison. Whether you want to see the Bengal tigers, two-toed tree sloth or De Brazza monkey exhibit, you are sure to appreciate the animals and their young.
If you are planning a trip near Halifax, PA, this summer, make sure to schedule a visit to Lake Tobias Wildlife Park. You can enjoy the baby animals along with the safari tours and reptile shows!

JR Tobias Museum & Education Center


Times

Museum opens every day at 11:00 a.m. and closes when the park closes

The award-winning J.R. Tobias Museum & Education Center tells the story of how a marine turned his family homestead into a sprawling zoo that became a favorite family attraction for over 180,000 park visitors each year. Using videos, photos, artifacts, and hands-on exhibits, the museum entertains and educates visitors on the making of the park, the founder’s love of wildlife, and how we can all contribute to the conservation of our world’s endangered wildlife. At its inception, the J.R. Tobias Museum & Education Center was awarded the New Attraction of the Year award by the Hershey Harrisburg Regional Visitor’s Bureau.

Animals on Display

In addition to the educational exhibits about the park and endangered animals, the museum also includes animal exhibits. While you’re here you can check out the beautiful toucans and a 500 gallon freshwater aquarium featuring jaquar and motoro stingrays, a silver arowana, and more.

Park Information


Our Vision

Here at Lake Tobias Wildlife Park, we believe in providing an accessible, affordable way for people to learn more about the natural world and the creatures that live in it. Interacting directly with our animals helps foster a love of nature that can last a lifetime, and enjoying wildlife makes a great family activity that creates a bond between generations. We’re a family-owned and -operated business, and we have more than 50 years of experience giving residents and visitors up-close encounters with wildlife.
